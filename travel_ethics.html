Travel Ethics

" Welcome to our simple and proven guide on how to be a responsible traveller in 2020. We believe that through the ways we travel, the regions we decide to visit, and the choices we make on how we spend our money and where , can have a positive impact on communities and families that are not as fortunate as ourselves.
Another name for it is ‘responsible travel'."

# Pack Light & Explore Your Own Backyard

  • Don't buy unnecessary travel items
  • Pack Light
  • Explore Your own country first , then travel abroad.

# Shop Locally

  • Your money can have a massive impact on the community.
  • Your money will help someone create a better life.
  • It will be a more authentic travel experience for yourself.

# Minimize your waste

  • Say no to plastic bags
  • Eat and drink in the cafe rather than going for take away
  • Carry a reusable water bottle with you and fill up where water is available .

# Respect the Local Culture

  • Learn about different cultures and religions.
  • Show respect to local customs and traditions.
  • Pre-study the local customs of the place , so that you don't inadvertently offend anyone.
  • Wear appropriate clothes.

# Don't give to beggars

  • Begging only encourages local people to continue asking tourists for money.
  • Often people pretend to be poor , so as to extort money from foreigners.
  • If you really want to help out, perhaps buy the person some food or water instead.

# Educate others to be Responsible Travellers

  • Some people are not aware of the implications their actions can have.
  • If you see someone unintentionally doing detrimental things then correct them in a friendly way.
  • Start discussions about responsible travel with people in your hostel.

" Remember, our actions can have a big impact on the world while we travel.
Make sure it is a positive one. "
